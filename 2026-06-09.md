- These are small/mid-cap, **violently volatile** names (aleabitoreddit says so
  himself); broad AI de-risking can swamp the thesis regardless of who's right on CPO.
- aleabitoreddit is **long the names he's pitching.**

**Tape read:** `@OddStats`

**Why it's interesting:** index-level vol is being mechanically suppressed by

**dispersion** lean (long single-name vol / short index vol). The concentration +
small-cap data backs the "fragile, narrow market" read.

**Ties to the report:** directly corroborated — NQ −5.2% vs RTY −2.4% wk/wk
(tech-specific de-rating, not broad risk-off) and VIX +5% to ~20.

**Risks:**

  index realized vol, long index vol just bleeds.
- The index-vs-single-name gap **can persist for a long time**; "cheap" isn't a
  catalyst by itself (CPI/FOMC are the catalysts that justify the timing).
- OddStats' historical stats are small-sample.
